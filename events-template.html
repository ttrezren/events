October 11, 2013 6:30pm - 8:30pm

The Inner World of Pina Bausch: What the Body Knows

German choreographer and director Pina Bausch engaged the personal and social experiences of body, space, time and energy. For Bausch, movement was not only a language; it was a palette of behavior that explored the contradictions of the personal body in relation to its social embodiments. Notably, the conversation between dreamer and the dream runs throughout Bausch’s non-linear approaches to narrative. At the same time, there are concrete elements in which each dancer is a distinct character, whose age, gender and other identity markers are not accidental. These characters, and their movements, leave behind searing visual images that linger long after their ephemeral gestures have faded from view. Choreographer Nina Haft will offer a window into the choreographic process of Pina Bausch and with the help of Orit Atzmon, we will link it to psychoanalytic ideas of creativity and the dream.

ECCC (Early Career Case Conference)

PINC Case Conferences for Early Career Clinicians meet weekly from Fall to Spring. The program is designed for early career clinicians who are seeking continued psychoanalytic development and community affiliation. The focus is on case presentation, clinical discussion and community building.

NPsA (Neuropsychoanalysis)

Neuro-psychoanalysis is a movement within neuroscience and psychoanalysis to combine the insights of both disciplines for a better understanding of mind and brain.

Open Source

An occasional series

Open To Interpretation (formerly Clinical Case Forum) Open to Interpretation is a newly conceived space for PINC's Community Members to connect in a relaxed setting and think collaboratively and with depth about issues of interest in today's world of psychoanalysis. The participants of each series will meet monthly for four months to explore, think and learn together about topics pertaining to psychoanalysis. Facilitated by two different analysts, the four meetings will alternate monthly between a theoretical discussion and a case presentation.
PINC Symposium PINC Symposium is a series that focuses on psychoanalytic ideas. While the format includes papers or lectures, the aim is to open dialogue and to engage with one another in a way that promotes vibrant thinking.
Second Fridays In this innovative monthly yearlong series we present programs with a fresh take on the intersection of psychoanalysis and the arts and culture. Programs emphasize dialogue, interaction, and discussion.
South Bay (SBCPS) Extended Study Series In this year’s Study Series, meeting weekly from September to April, four analysts will present vignettes and discuss their ideas about emotional attention, intervention, interpretation and interpretive action.
OPIC Events Sponsored by the Outreach and Public Information Committee
Visiting Scholars Each year, the Visiting Scholars program for the PINC community invites scholars from around the country and around the world to present their more current ideas and engage in a stimulating discussion with the PINC community. These events are offered to enrich the intellectual and clinical life of our community and all PINC members are invited to attend.
